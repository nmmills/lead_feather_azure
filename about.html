Meet our Camp Families

Mills Family

Sara, Nicole and Morgan

Nicole's introduction to the Alafia River Rendezvous © was through sending her husband and children to enjoy the event every January. However, in 2020, following his passing, Nicole attended as a day visitor and quickly formed connections with many people. Encouraged to join, she became a member, leading to the strengthening of her friendship with Michelle.

Stefani Family

Michelle, Simon, Ruby and Chalis

Michelle and Nicole's paths first crossed when they were homeschooling their children. Michelle had the unique experience of growing up watching her father participate in the Florida Frontiersmen club. She often joined him in shooting muzzleloaders and throwing hawks on Shoot Weekends. Together Michelle and Nicole, partnered up in 2022 and established a new camp within the Alafia River Rendezvous ©, which has now grown to include 6 families.

Baker Family

Steve, Kristen, Nolan and Lincoln

In 2023, the Baker family, also a homeschooled family, joined our camp.

Our family started participating in the Alafia River Rendezvous after touring the event with a homeschool group. Our boys started begging to be a part of this living history event, and I knew we needed to support that. They wait all year for this one week where we can slow down, learn lost skills, and deepen our knowledge of history. The families in our encampment have become dear friends that we look forward to spending time with year after year. - Kristen and Steve Baker

Robson Family

Dave and Karson

As for the Robson family, they first visited as day guests in 2023, only to fully commit and stay overnight in our camper in 2024. They wholeheartedly embraced the diverse learning experiences and enjoyable activities that the camp has to offer.
